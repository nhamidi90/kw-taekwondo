What is Taekwondo?

The martial art, taekwondo (태권도), was founded in Korea in 1966 by General Choi Hong-Hi. word is broken down into 'Tae' (foot), 'Kwon' (fist) and 'Do (way)' so you get the meaning 'the art of the hand and foot'. It consists of punching techniques but the focus is mostly on kicking. Students will learn a mix of learning basic techniques (strikes, and blocks), patterns and sparring as well as breaking, step-sparring and self-defence patterns. There are different federations of taekwondo but we follow the UK International Taekwondo Federation (UK ITF)

What are the tenets of taekwondo?

Courtesy - 예의

Courtesy (ye-wee in korean) means showing politeness in your attitude to others at all times.

Integrity - 염치

Integrity (Yom-chee in korean) involves determining between morally right and wrong and also standing up for what you believe is correct.

Perseverance - 인내

Perseverance (In-nae in korean) is the detemination to carry on doing something without giving up despite it being difficilt.

Self Control - 극기

Self-control (Geuk-gi in korean) is the ability to control one's emotions and actions, particularly in trying situations.

Indomitable Spirit - 백절불굴

If someone has an Indomitable spirit (Baek-jul-bool-gool in korean), they never give into defeat. They are strong-willed and have the hope and courage to tackle their obstacles.

What is grading?

As you develop your techniques in taekwondo you may want to go through the grading system. The colour of your belt changes as you progress. After you reach 1st Kup, you will then be ready to move onto the black belt (Dan) grading system.

Kup Colour belt
10th kup White belt
9th kup White belt with yellow stripe
8th kup Yellow belt
7th kup Yellow belt with green stripe
6th kup Green belt
5th kup Green belt with blue stripe
4th kup Blue belt
3rd kup Blue belt with red stripe
2nd kup Red belt
1st kup Red belt with black stripe
